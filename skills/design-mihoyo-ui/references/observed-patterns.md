# Observed official web patterns

Research date: 2026-07-23. These are design observations and implementation inferences, not an official brand guide. Campaign pages change; verify live pages when exact current fidelity matters.

## Source set

- miHoYo corporate site: https://www.mihoyo.com/
- HoYoverse corporate site: https://www.hoyoverse.com/en-us/
- Honkai: Star Rail official site: https://hsr.hoyoverse.com/en-us/
- HoYoLAB community product: https://www.hoyolab.com/home?lang=en-us

## miHoYo corporate site

### Observed

- Opens as a full-viewport experience rather than a conventional document page.
- Centers the stacked statement `TECH OTAKUS SAVE THE WORLD` in very heavy white sans-serif.
- Colors selected letter segments cyan, making technology the signal accent inside the typography.
- Places the statement over dark, muted moving imagery and a restrained rectangular play control.
- Keeps the white logo small in the upper-left and uses a minimal scroll cue below the hero.
- Later content uses more conventional light editorial sections and a cyan corporate accent.

### Apply as

- Use one declarative headline as the visual anchor.
- Create emphasis inside typography rather than adding decorative badges.
- Keep navigation and secondary controls visually quiet during the cinematic opening.
- Transition from dark brand impact to readable product content instead of maintaining maximum drama everywhere.

## HoYoverse corporate site

### Observed

- Uses a fixed black navigation bar with white text and an ice-blue active state/underline.
- Leads with a full-bleed product carousel: title and short message on the left, position/thumbnail controls toward the edge.
- Alternates immersive black/media sections with white editorial grids for updates and product cards.
- Uses blue as the consistent interaction signal across navigation, links, and consent actions.
- Keeps typography primarily bold, neutral sans-serif so campaign imagery carries the personality.
- Uses modest card rounding in editorial content rather than highly ornamental chrome.

### Apply as

- Separate the brand canvas from information architecture.
- Keep active navigation obvious with one thin, bright gesture.
- Use quiet cards and strong image crops in content-heavy sections.
- Let a single accent color unify otherwise varied product imagery.

## Honkai: Star Rail official site

### Observed

- Builds the page from large, fixed-ratio illustrated scenes with layered foreground, character art, and decorative UI.
- Combines dark navy/indigo panels, luminous violet, pale paper-white fields, and fine warm-gold frames.
- Uses asymmetric compositions: large character art occupies one side while quotations, labels, or selection controls occupy the other.
- Repeats star, diamond, orbit, ring, tick, and clipped-corner motifs.
- Uses circular character selectors, framed quotations, compact version labels, and numbered carousels.
- Transitions between saturated illustrated zones and very light negative-space zones instead of keeping one flat background.
- Uses serif/script accents as flavor while readable sans-serif handles utility copy.
- Animates scene layers and page transitions more strongly than individual buttons.

### Apply as

- Use cosmic motifs as structural diagrams: routes, propagation paths, version timelines, or regional orbits.
- Reserve ornate framing for the selected event, character, or decision.
- Pair a large narrative object with compact structured evidence.
- Alternate atmosphere and clarity: immersive overview, then calm analysis, then decisive action.

## HoYoLAB community product

### Observed from public page structure

- Organizes a high-volume feed by game channel and content type.
- Keeps repeated utility actions such as follow, filter, tools, check-in, notifications, and topic discovery close to content.
- Surfaces topic activity, members, views, replies, and reactions as compact metadata.
- Supports the same information architecture across Chinese, English, and Japanese locales.

### Apply as

- Treat community data as a product surface, not a cinematic scene.
- Use compact metadata, clear filters, and stable repeated card anatomy.
- Keep localized navigation and metric labels flexible enough for language expansion.

## Synthesis for operational dashboards

The reusable pattern is not “purple sci-fi cards.” It is a contrast between:

1. **World-building:** one large visual or live state establishes context.
2. **Instrument layer:** fine lines, clipped frames, small labels, and status signals explain the system.
3. **Editorial clarity:** evidence and updates sit on calm, readable surfaces.
4. **Human decision:** one high-contrast action remains visually unmistakable.

Use this sequence to style game operations, publishing, localization, live-service, or community intelligence tools without sacrificing usability.

## Originality boundary

The official sites are references, not component libraries. Reuse abstract principles—contrast, hierarchy, cinematic-to-editorial pacing, asymmetric composition, and semantic accents. Do not reproduce logos, campaign illustrations, proprietary typefaces, exact ornamental frames, or a recognizably identical hero layout without explicit rights.
